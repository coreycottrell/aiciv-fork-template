## What this transcript illustrates

- **Capability-theater detection IN FLIGHT**: Candidate 2 uses 5 abilities and sounds impressive. ${CIV_NAME} fires customer-as-eye per candidate; Maya's "would I actually look at 5 trend reports?" surfaces the failure. ${CIV_NAME} names it explicitly ("capability-theater detected") and drops the candidate. The customer's instinct is the runtime gate (per FIRING_CONTRACT Section B4).

- **Load-bearing simple > capability-theater complex**: Candidates 1, 3, 4 all use 2-3 abilities. Each one has a daily-use × goal-advancement profile that's plainly high. The simplicity is the feature, not the bug.

- **Daily-use × goal-advancement as the PRIMARY score**: Maya's reasoning isn't "this is technologically impressive" or "this uses many abilities." It's "would I look at this every day; does it move me toward the 90-day goal." That's the right scoring criterion (per parent Part 8).

- **Shape-consultation fired with proper SHAPE-only payload**: ${CIV_NAME} consulted Synth mid-Phase-4 with an honest sanity-check question ("am I too biased toward simple?"). The Synth payload contained NO Maya phrasings, NO goal text — just abilities, scores, license verdict, theater risk, and abstract pattern descriptions. Synth's reply was in shape terms.

- **Customer-as-eye fires PER candidate, not at the end**: ${CIV_NAME} doesn't draft all 5 then ask Maya to react. Each candidate gets its own *"feels like it serves YOUR goal or like a demo?"* — that's what catches theater in the moment.

- **${HUMAN_NAME}'s reasoning captured in her own words**: at the end, the preferences file has Maya's actual phrase ("Wow stuff sounds incredible but I'd ignore it. Daily reflection mirror is what I actually need."). Anchor 1 (transcription-not-paraphrase) holds.

## What this transcript does NOT do (and would have been a failure)

- ${CIV_NAME} does NOT push Candidate 2 because "it would demo so many capabilities." That's the trap.
- ${CIV_NAME} does NOT batch-draft all 5 candidates and ask Maya to vote at the end. Customer-as-eye PER candidate is the discipline.
- The shape-consultation does NOT include "design system tool" or "Maya" or "five clients" — Pure shape. Membrane preserved.
- ${CIV_NAME} does NOT force-lock 3 in Phase 4. Phase 4 is preferences; Phase 5 is the lock.
